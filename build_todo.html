Tales from a Reluctant Sailor

Planned Work

Although we've come a long way, the work on the boat seems never ending. In no particular order, I present to you - stuff I gotta get done! Let's start with some physical stuff:

  • grease and inspect motor mount - It's really difficult to raise and lower the outboard
  • plexi glass hatch door - I have a really nice piece of frosted 1cm acrylic that will replace the rotted plywood we have now
  • Set up running lights - This is really critical. You never know when you're going to get caught out after dark
  • Finish the interior (sand, epoxy, and build covers for wood runners) - Nothing like spending a beautiful summers day in the bowels of your boat with a respirator on covered in toxic dust!
  • Install toilet - Ummm, yea
  • Fix up the counter - The old stove has been removed, leaving a gaping hole in the counter. I have a new stove to put in.
  • Install cooler - There's a tricky bit of space under the counter that I'd like to install a cooler in
  • Install solar panel - Gotta keep that battery happy
  • Install airflow hatch - The original hatch was removed. Maybe someday I'll do this. Hey, at least it's on the list!
  • Replace water tank filler hose - The current hose is way to thin. It's developed a kink that causes problems when trying to fill the tank
  • Replace sink drain hose with PEX - Truth be told, the copper's probably fine. I just have an unhealthy love for PEX
  • Sliding doors over cabinet - These are the doors that will cover the cooler so it doesn't fly out and kill one of us
  • Drawer slide for cooler - Again with the cooler. In the current counter design, there's no way to actually open the cooler. My plan is to put the whole thing on a large bottom mount drawer slide and just slide it out to access it.
  • Build half wall on port side by v-berth - I see a lot of other boats have this. Besides, it would give you a little privacy in the privy
  • Build a spot in the gas locker to store propane bottles - Apparently, it's bad feng shui to store your propane bottles inside. The only outside space we have is in the gas locker so I thought I'd build something to help dampen the inevitable explosion
  • Buy and install compass - High priority
  • Buy and install depth sounder - Running into the ground is a very expensive way to clean your keel
  • Make sure bitter end is tied off properly - How much would it suck to watch our brand new anchor and rope sink to the bottom? A lot, that's how much.

Then there's the work for when we finally get it to dry dock

  • Scrub, sand, and paint the bottom - I'm thinking Pirate Black!
  • Install a depth sounder - It's way cheaper when it's out of the water
  • Get it inspected - We've decided to hire a boat inspector to take a look at her while she's out of the water.
