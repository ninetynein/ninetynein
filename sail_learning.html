Tales from a Reluctant Sailor

Safety First!

I don't really have a lot to offer at this point, but I have learned a few very important things so far in this adventure. The two most important things you need are knowledge and equipment (duh). I can't stress enough how important it is fully comprehend the gravitas of being on the water. I really believe the government does us a great disservice to us by making the Pleasure Craft Operator's License the only requirement for taking out a boat. It lures us into thinking that if we can pass this minimal test, we're prepaired for the water. This is NOT the case.

Of course, the knowledge isn't much good with the gear! Here's the barebones, Canadian legal requirements for a boat of our size (20' to 30'), as per Ace Boater website:

  1. One (1) Canadian-approved personal flotation device or lifejacket of appropriate size for each person on board
  2. One (1) buoyant heaving line at least than 15 m (49’3”) long OR One (1) lifebuoy attached to a buoyant line at least 15 m (49’3”) long
  3. One (1) reboarding device (Note: A reboarding device is only required if the vertical height that must be climbed to reboard the pleasure craft from the water is over 0.5 m (1’8”).)
  4. One (1) watertight flashlight
  5. Six (6) Canadian-approved flares of Type A (Rocket Parachute), B (Multi-Star) or C (Hand) (Note: Not required for racing or rivers, canals, or lakes where you can't be more than 1 nautical mile from shore)
  6. One (1) manual propelling device or One (1) anchor and at least 15 m (49’3”) of cable, rope or chain in any combination
  7. One (1) bailer or manual bilge pump (Note: not required for boats that can't hold enough water to sink)
  8. One (1) sound-signalling device
  9. Navigation lights that meet the requirements set out in the Collision Regulations (Note: Only required if sailing at night or in restricted visibility)
  10. One (1) magnetic compass
  11. One (1) 5BC fire extinguisher if the pleasure craft is equipped with a motor
  12. One (1) 5BC fire extinguisher if the pleasure craft is equipped with a fuel-burning cooking, heating or refrigeration appliance
  13. One (1) radar reflector is required under certain conditions
